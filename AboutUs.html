The Velvetones (About Us)

Forward: Biographical Backgound:


The Velvetones were born as a duo in 1960 when Norman Paraschuk and Brian Kelly met in a high school music class.
Ever since that day, the world has been treated to the fruits of their combined musical talents.
With both Paraschuk and Kelly composing their own vibrant and fresh material, their songs have captured the flavour of their youth and the dreams of their millions of ardent fans.
This collection gives special tribute to the creative genius of Norman Paraschuk, but is just the tip of the iceberg that is the totally “ cool “ sound of The Velvetones.
So hold tight, get ready to dance, and prepare to be blown away because “ The Velvetones Sing Tonight. “ - Dick Clark

Song Inspirations

  • Selections Valerie, Margie, Joanna, Eva and Julia Baby chronicle a young man's typical infatuation with pretty girls that populate a young man's life. Like the girls themselves, the songs take hold of the innocence, passion and fererent emotions of those times.
  • All in One Blue Day is a brilliant microcosm of the trials, tribulations and woes that can beset any young man. Possibly inspired by Eddie Cochran's “ Summertime Blues “ ( several scholars disagree ) this song goes much farther than Cochran in establishing the anguish that can erupt from incidents gone wrong in a young man's life. 
  • Selections I told You Once: I told You Twice and I'm Gonna Love You All My Life are representative songs of the driving beat that is so much a part of Norman Paraschuk's music, - an irrepressible, uncontrollable, force that sends fans into a frenzy of toe-tappin', feet stompin' wild dancing!
  • Selection This Pain in My Heart follows the vein of All in One Blue Day, except that it centres upon the excruciating pain that comes from a love gone wrong and can send one into metaphorical tears, if not the real thing. The pain that Paraschuk captures is almost exotic.
  • Selection Lonely Nights was the proposed first LP of The Velvetones, but it was never recorded until late in the duo's career. Being the title song of this collection, it is arguably the crown jewel of Norman Paraschuk's ingenuity and creative mastery. Several other mainline artists ( most noteably Elvis Presley, Jerry Lee Lewis and Buddy Holly ) have attempted to do cover versions of Lonely Nights, but none of them could capture Paraschuk's magic touch, and so all cover versions were subsequently and wisely dropped.
  • The Velvetones Sing Tonight is the duo's signature song that lights the match to The Velvetones' fireworks. It features a live performance on The Ed Sullivan Show.
  • Partner Brian Kelly wrote and performed Norman's Squeeze Box as a tribute to his life-long friend and as a nostalgic look back to how it all happened for The Velvetones.
  • Down Memory Lane, Parkdale P.C.I., & Listen to Norm combine the melodies of Gary “ US Bonds' “ New Orleans, The Village Stompers Washington Square and Buddy Holly's “ Listen to Me “ with lyrics offering further tribute to Velvetone, Norman Paraschuk.
  • When You Ask About Love This “ cover “ version of The Crickets' great song is one of the most requested songs whenever The Velvetones perform. No collection of their works would be complete without its inclusion.
  • Fedora Using the Otis Blackwell melody to Elvis Presley's famous “ Don't Be Cruel, “ Kelly adapted the song to fit Norman Paraschuk's wife, Fedora, in a tribute to his favourite couple.

Noteable Performances

  • May 13, 1959  The Lawrence Welk Show
    When introducing The Velvetones, Welk told the audience:
    “ When..ummmbummm.. both Velvetones sing... ummmbummmm,.. you get a-one-and-a-two-and-a-one-and-a-two...ummmmbummmmm “
  • March 2, 1960  The Ed Sullivan Show
    This was the largest studio audience to that date. Fans had to be turned away in droves causing massive traffic tie-ups and a clogged city that lasted well into the night.Ed Sullivan commented that “ It was a really big show! “
  • May 13, 1960  The Apollo Theater
    This extravaganza included a special guest appearance by Fats Domino on piano.The audience was heavily populated by rock stars including Sam Cooke who told reporters “ They send me, “ and Clarence “ Frogman “ Henry who admitted “ I don't know why I love them , but I do. “ Chuck Berry said enthusiastically, “ Many people came from miles around to hear them play their music till the sun goes down.''
  • March 2, 1961 Carnegie Hall
    The Velvetones were backed up by the Cleveland Philharmonic Orchestra during this formal attire affair. Renowned pianists Glen Gould and Liberace shared duties on the piano and Lawrence Welk made a cameo appearance on accordion. 
  • May 13, 1961 The Whitehouse
    JFK's entire cabinete joined the president and first lady in attendance, after which President Kennedy said to the TV audience: Ask not what more The Velvetones can do for you, but what you can now do for The Velvetones. The Velvetones were then presented with the Keys to the city of Washington.
  • March 2, 1962 The Kremlin
    Regaled as the single greatest achievement from a Democratic society, The Soviet Union accepted The Velvetones as one of their own. Upon completion of their sensational hit, Eva, President Nikita Kruschev lept to his feet, pounded the podium with his shoe, and shouted: “ Da! Da! Da! “
  • May 13, 1962 Command Performance at Buckingham Palace
    Against royal protocal, HRM Queen Elizabeth and Prince Phillip broke into a spontaneous Jitterbug together when The Velvetones sang I'm Gonna Love You All My life. Not to be outdone, Prince Charles and Princess Ann began to Charleston to The Velvetone's masterpiece, The Velvetones Sing Tonight. When being presented to the Royal Family at the conclusion of the evening, The Queen exclaimed “ Jolly Good Show” Prince Phillip added “Ripping!! “
  • While millions upon millions of rock fans flock to the concerts of such Superstars as The Velvetones, Buddy Holly & The Crickets, Elvis Presley, Fats Dominio, Jerry Lee Lewis, Ricky Nelson, Bo Diddley, Carl Perkins, and Little Richard, - who is that these great stars themselves pine to see perform? The Velvetones and Buddy Holly & The Crickets have formed an “ Admiration Society “ and go to each other's concerts on a regular basis. Rock insiders know that the two main performers that attract stars are The Velvetones and Jackie Wilson, - and Jackie Wilson seldoms misses a Velvetones' appearance.
